Lecture 1 - Introduction to Physics
MCQs
1. What is the scientific method based on?
Answer: b) Reason, logic, and experimental evidence
2. What are the three fundamental dimensions in physics?
Answer: b) Mass, Length, Time
3. The dimensional formula for Force is:
Answer: b) MLT⁻²
Short Questions
Q: What is the difference between units and dimensions?
Dimensions are fundamental quantities (M, L, T) while units are the standards used to measure them.
Q: Why is dimensional analysis important?
It helps verify equations, convert units, and check the validity of physical relationships.
Lecture 2 - Kinematics I
MCQs
1. Displacement is:
Answer: c) The position of a body at time t
2. For constant acceleration, final velocity equation is:
Answer: b) v = v₀ + at
3. The equation x = x₀ + v₀t + ½at² is valid for:
Answer: b) Constant acceleration
Short Questions
Q: What is the difference between speed and velocity?
Speed is scalar (rate of distance), velocity is vector (rate of displacement with direction).
Q: Can a body have zero velocity but non-zero acceleration?
Yes, e.g., at the highest point of a thrown ball, velocity is zero but gravity acts downward.
Lecture 3 - Kinematics II
MCQs
1. The derivative of x = t² with respect to time is:
Answer: b) 2t
2. If x = tⁿ, then dx/dt equals:
Answer: b) ntⁿ⁻¹
3. The acceleration due to gravity near Earth's surface is approx:
Answer: b) 9.8 m/s²
Short Questions
Q: Define derivative in physical terms.
A derivative shows how fast a function changes. Velocity is dx/dt, acceleration is dv/dt.
Q: What is the scalar product of two vectors?
A·B = AB cosθ. It equals the length of one vector times the projection of the other.
Lecture 4 - Force and Newton's Laws
MCQs
1. Newton's First Law states that:
Answer: b) Remains at rest/uniform motion unless acted by external force
2. The SI unit of force is:
Answer: c) Newton
3. According to Newton's Third Law:
Answer: b) F_AB = -F_BA
Short Questions
Q: State Newton's Second Law.
F = ma. The net force on a body equals its mass times its acceleration.
Q: What is the difference between mass and weight?
Mass is constant matter. Weight is gravitational force (W=mg) and varies.
Lecture 5 - Applications of Newton's Laws I
MCQs
1. For a body in equilibrium:
Answer: c) F = 0
2. The coefficient of friction μ is:
Answer: a) Force/Normal force
3. For a massless rope, tension:
Answer: c) Is same everywhere
Short Questions
Q: What is static equilibrium?
Body at rest with sum of all forces zero (ΣF = 0).
Q: How does friction depend on normal force?
Friction is directly proportional to normal force (F = μN).
Lecture 6 - Applications of Newton's Laws II
MCQs
1. Fluid resistance force typically:
Answer: c) Increases with speed
2. Terminal velocity is reached when:
Answer: b) Frictional force equals gravitational force
3. In a lift accelerating upward, you feel:
Answer: b) Heavier
Short Questions
Q: What is terminal velocity?
Constant maximum velocity reached when drag force equals gravitational force.
Lecture 7 - Work and Energy
MCQs
1. Work is defined as:
Answer: b) Force × displacement (in direction of force)
2. The SI unit of work is:
Answer: c) Joule
3. Kinetic energy of a body is:
Answer: d) ½mv²
Short Questions
Q: Define work in physics.
Work W = F·d = Fd cosθ. Energy transferred by a force.
Q: What is power?
Power is the rate of doing work (P = Work/Time). Unit is Watt.
Lecture 8 - Conservation of Energy
MCQs
1. Potential energy is:
Answer: b) Stored energy
2. Gravitational potential energy is:
Answer: a) mgh
3. Energy stored in a spring is:
Answer: d) ½kx²
Q: State the law of conservation of energy.
Energy cannot be created or destroyed. Total mechanical energy is constant without friction.
Lecture 9 - Momentum
MCQs
1. Momentum is defined as:
Answer: a) Mass × velocity
2. The unit of momentum is:
Answer: b) kg·m/s
3. Impulse equals:
Answer: b) Change in momentum
Short Questions
Q: What is impulse?
Impulse I = F·Δt = Δp, the change in momentum.
Lecture 10 - Collisions
MCQs
1. In elastic collision:
Answer: c) Both momentum and KE are conserved
2. In perfectly inelastic collision:
Answer: a) Objects stick together
Short Questions
Q: Difference between elastic and inelastic collisions?
Elastic: Momentum & KE conserved. Inelastic: Only Momentum conserved.
Lecture 11 - Rotational Kinematics
MCQs
1. One complete revolution equals:
Answer: b) 2π radians
2. Relationship between linear and angular velocity:
Answer: a) v = rω
Short Questions
Q: Define angular velocity.
Rate of change of angular displacement (ω = dθ/dt).
Lecture 12 - Physics of Many Particles
MCQs
1. Centre of mass is defined as:
Answer: a) Σm_i r_i / M
2. For rotational motion, Moment of Inertia (I) is analogous to:
Answer: c) Mass
Short Questions
Q: What is moment of inertia?
Rotational analog of mass (I = Σmr²), measuring resistance to rotation.
Lecture 13 - Angular Momentum
MCQs
1. Angular momentum L is defined as:
Answer: a) r × p
2. Angular momentum is conserved when:
Answer: b) Torque is zero
Short Questions
Q: State law of conservation of angular momentum.
If net external torque is zero, total angular momentum remains constant.
Lecture 14 - Equilibrium of Rigid Bodies
MCQs
1. For static equilibrium:
Answer: c) Both ΣF = 0 and Στ = 0
2. Centre of gravity is:
Answer: b) Point where weight acts
Short Questions
Q: Conditions for static equilibrium?
Sum of forces = 0 and Sum of torques = 0.
Lecture 15 - Oscillations I
MCQs
1. For SHM, the restoring force is:
Answer: b) F = -kx
2. Angular frequency ω for spring-mass:
Answer: b) √(k/m)
Short Questions
Q: What is simple harmonic motion?
Motion where restoring force is proportional to displacement (F = -kx).
Lecture 16 - Oscillations II
MCQs
1. For a simple pendulum, ω equals:
Answer: b) √(g/L)
2. Resonance occurs when:
Answer: b) Driving frequency equals natural frequency
Short Questions
Q: What causes damping?
Dissipative forces like friction or air resistance that remove energy.
Lecture 17 - Physics of Materials
MCQs
1. Elasticity is:
Answer: b) Tendency to regain original shape
2. Hooke's Law states:
Answer: b) Stress = Constant × Strain
Short Questions
Q: Define stress and strain.
Stress = Force/Area. Strain = Fractional deformation.
Lecture 18 - Physics of Fluids
MCQs
1. Pascal's principle states:
Answer: b) Pressure in enclosed fluid is transmitted equally
2. Pressure at depth h is:
Answer: b) P₀ + ρgh
Short Questions
Q: State Bernoulli's equation significance.
P + ½ρv² + ρgy = constant. Shows higher velocity regions have lower pressure.
Lecture 19 - Physics of Sound
MCQs
1. Sound waves are:
Answer: b) Longitudinal waves
2. Doppler effect occurs due to:
Answer: b) Relative motion between source and observer
Short Questions
Q: Explain Doppler effect.
Source approaching = higher frequency/pitch. Source receding = lower frequency.
Lecture 20 - Wave Motion
MCQs
1. In longitudinal waves, particle motion is:
Answer: b) Parallel to wave direction
2. For constructive interference, phase difference is:
Answer: b) 2π or 0
Short Questions
Q: Difference between transverse and longitudinal waves?
Transverse: Oscillation perpendicular to propagation. Longitudinal: Oscillation parallel.

MCQs
1. Newton's law of gravitation states F ∝:

A line joining a planet to the Sun sweeps out equal areas in equal time intervals.
